Session 2: History

 

 

The World Wide Web debuted in 1990. Since the Web's inception, three major iterations of technology propelled features and services that are now ubiquitously available like social media, artificial intelligence, and cryptocurrency. In this session in addition to reviewing these technology iterations, we will look at a timeline of events that showcases the emergence of sites and services related to upcoming sessions and illuminates how they intersect.

Discussion Prompts

This session will be entirely "Lecture + Q&A". However, participants are encouraged to consider on the following questions prior to this session. Reflecting on personal "firsts" related to use of the Internet and Web is an effective way to place the history of the Web into a personal context.

  1. When did you get your first home computer?
  2. When did you first go online? If your first online experience was at your place of business, when did you first subscribe to an ISP at home?
  3. When did you get your first smartphone?
  4. When did you shop online for the first time? Was it through Amazon or some other e-business?
  5. What was the first commercial streaming service that you subscribed to and when did you start that subscription?
