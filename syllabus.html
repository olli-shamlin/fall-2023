Syllabus

Goals

This course aims to enhance students' understanding of the Web and select associated technologies through lectures, classroom discussions, and independent study opportunities. We will increase our understanding of the scale of the Web via reviews of statistics and demographic studies; we will survey the history of the Web to better understand the pace of the evolution of associated technology; we will review human rights associated with digital life.

With these general contexts in hand, we will survey select types of services hosted on the Web and have facilitated discussions on their impact on society. In addition to discussion prompts prepared by the instructor, students will have the opportunity to influence discussions by proposing topics and questions of their own for group consideration.

Web resources will be provided that students can use at their discretion to further their learning. Freely accessible websites and articles related to each session's theme will be shared by the instructor on the course website for students to study at their discretion and based on their learning style and preferences.

Session Format

Each ninety-minute session is divided into two primary parts:

  1. Lecture

    All lecture content is intended for a general audience. No pre-existing familiarity with any of the session topics is required. Additional recommended resources students can use to extend their understanding beyond what is covered during classroom lectures can be found on each session specific page.

  2. Group Discussion

    Instructor proposed discussion prompts for each session can be found on each session specific page.

    Note
    • Instructor proposed prompts are intended to initiate or stimulate group discussion only if needed. Students are welcome and encouraged to share additional or alternative topics and questions. This can be done either during the sessions themselves or by contacting the instructor at the email address provided above prior to a session.

See the Materials section below for additional information about session specific pages.

Approximately half of each session's allotted time will be given to each. Ie, 40-45 minutes per section. We will take a short (five minute) break between parts during each session.

Policies and Conduct

While we will uphold Policies, practicing our community's values of Curiosity, Connection, Kindness, and Compassion; these sentences from our Code of Conduct appropriately reflect the attitude all participates are expected to have: We welcome a lively and passionate exchange of ideas and perspectives. Opposing viewpoints are welcomed and appreciated.

Instructor and students need to foster a classroom atmosphere of trust and respect in order to maximize the value and benefit we receive during group discussions.

Furthermore, web-related technologies that we will survey during the course can be complex.
