Session 1: Size and Shape

 

 

The World Wide Web is massively large. In this session we will review statistics that describe the size of the Web and see how researchers have visualized the Web to gain a better appreciation for its magnitude and scale. We will also look at demographics related to users and uses of the Web.

Discussion Prompts

Given this is our first session, we will use our group discussion time to introduce ourselves and briefly share about our individual attitudes towards the Web using the following questions:

  1. What is the greatest benefit you get from the Web?
  2. What is your biggest frustration or concern when using the Web?
  3. What are you most interested in getting from this course?

Discussion Summary

In this session, each course participant shared one or more aspects of the Web they considered (1) a benefit, (2) a frustration or concern, and (3) something(s) they would like to gain from the class. From the instructor's perspective, four major themes can be distilled from the various frustrations and concerns shared by participants:

  • Trust


    As we learned during this session's lecture, there are on the order of two billion sites on the Web, and (unfortunately) some should not be trusted. Cyber criminals do create websites to scam and exploit others for illicit purposes; some sites may promote misinformation and/or disinformation. Multiple participants are interested in learning effective ways to discern the trustworthiness of a website as well as the credibility of the information found on websites.

  • Navigation


    We also learned during this session's lecture that there is a massive amount of information spread across the web. While all participants are familiar with search engines, some expressed interest in learning ways to use them more effectively. Also, some participants expressed frustration related to navigating websites. A website can offer numerous capabilities and/or a trove of information; website content is typically organized across multiple pages; effectively navigating within a site can be challenging at times. Additionally, sometimes we discover a page we want to return to later; while participants are generally aware of web browsers' bookmarking capabilities, several participants expressed interest in gaining a better understanding of how to create and manage bookmarks.

  • Privacy


    One student directly express strong concern of the common practice among websites of collecting/retaining personal information and "tracking information." The instructor observed multiple other participants resonating with this observation. I.e., there was a log of "head nodding." Others raised concerns/frustrations related to web cookies. (Cookies are one of the techniques websites use to track and "remember" what a person does when visiting a website.)

  • Well-being


    More than one participant indicated awareness of the potential to develop addictive behaviors while using the Web; others also indicated awareness of other mental health concerns (e.g., depression, anxiety, self-esteem) related to social media.

The instructor considers the above the most significant themes within this session's discussions for two key reasons: (1) multiple participants expressed them or indicated they resonated with them, and (2) they strongly align with the course's scope. As we progress through the course, the instructor will aim to further explore each of the above in more detail and in pragmatic ways that we can incorporate into our daily use of the web.

The instructor did take note of a number of other concerns raised:

  • New technology being "forced on me"
  • Understanding "the cloud"
  • Dealing with ads
  • Managing email
  • Deleting website accounts I know longer need/use
  • Managing subscription services
  • Ability to create PDFs

More than one person expressed frustration with ads; the other items in the above list appeared to the instructor to be more isolated concerns/frustrations among participants.
