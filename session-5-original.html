Session 5: Search & Cloud

 

 

Since Bitcoin's debut in 2009, the cryptocurrency market has grown to include more than 9,900 different types of cryptocurrencies with a global capitalization of 1.17 trillion USD and has unfolded with numerous controversies and scandals. In this session, we will review how related technologies work and how they are expanding to other use cases like non-fungible tokens (NFTs), supply chain management, and shipping logistics.

Discussion Prompts

  1. Cryptocurrency software enables trusted transactions to be conducted without the need for a central authority. Do you think there are strong advantages to such a model when conducting personal business or do you prefer consider the central authority model more reliable and trustworthy?
  2. Cryptocurrency has roots in libertarian ideology. Do you think there should be greater government regulation for cryptocurrencies?
  3. Are there any situations where you might prefer to use cryptocurrency instead of your "traditional" banking accounts to do business? Do you think blockchain technology applied to other kinds of transactions (e.g., real estate or voting) could be improvements to current practices?
