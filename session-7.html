Session 7: The Dark Web & Cybersecurity

 

 

Note: The instructor originally planned to provide an overview of only the Dark Web in this session; an overview of cybersecurity originally planned to be covered in the eighth and final session was added to this session with consensus of course participants.

Many people imagine that the Dark Web is a mysterious corner of the Internet where illicit activity occurs. In addition to clarifying what and where the Dark Web is, in this session we will examine how it is accessed; examples of Dark Web activities, sites, and services; and the type of Web users often encountered there: hackers.

As the Web has advanced, so has the frequency and sophistication of attacks against digital systems by bad actors; malicious hackers use malware (like viruses and ransomware) to compromise systems--sometimes to make them inoperable and sometimes to steal digital data assets. Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, and software systems from digital attacks.
