Session 4: Social Media

 

 

Social media is the most common use of the Web. Researchers estimate that 4.9 billion people worldwide will use social media sites and spend approximately two and a half hours on social media platforms every day. In this session we will review social media demographics in more detail and survey the types of social media sites and common features of social media platforms. We will also discuss the nature and controversies of algorithms used to populate user feeds and social graphs.

Discussion Prompts

  1. What is something that bothers you about social media? What changes could social media companies make to address your concern?
  2. Do you think social media is a good source for news? If so, do you think misinformation, dis-information, and "fake news" on social media companies have a responsibility to assure misinformation, dis-information, and "fake news" are not disseminated on their platforms?
  3. Do you think social media is bad for mental health (including addictive behavior)? If so, what steps can/should we take to manage our mental well being when using social media? Should social media companies take steps to reduce mental health risks? If so, what would you like to see social media companies do?
  4. Do you have concerns about your personal privacy when using social media sites? If so, what steps do you take to protect your privacy?
